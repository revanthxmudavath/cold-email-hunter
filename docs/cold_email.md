# Cold Email Writing Guide

## Rules
- Subject: 7 words or fewer, role-specific (e.g. "Backend Engineer role at Stripe")
- Body: 120-180 words total — count carefully, no exceptions
- Tone: direct, professional, fully human — never mention AI or automation
- Every email MUST include the exact job URL from the job posting
- NO em dashes (—) anywhere in the email — use a comma, period, or rewrite the sentence instead
- NO special characters that look odd in plain text email clients

## Template

Subject: [Role] at [Company]

Hi [First Name],

[One sentence: who you are + ONE specific company hook. A product they shipped,
a tech detail you noticed, or something concrete about what they build. Not generic praise.]

Given my background in [relevant area], I think [Company] would be a great fit:

- Architected 30+ REST API endpoints supporting 50K users with AWS Cognito auth (StylePilot.ai)
- Cut search latency 75% (8s to under 2s) via dual B-tree + GIN indexing on PostgreSQL JSONB
- Raised backend test coverage 45% to 78% at SpeedScore and established team-wide TDD standards
- Built event-driven microservices processing 60+ articles/min (RaveDigest, FastAPI + Redis Streams)

Roles I'm interested in:
[Job Title]: [EXACT job URL from job posting]

Would love to connect and happy to share my resume if my background looks relevant.

Best,
Revanth Mudavath
revanthmudavath2001@gmail.com | linkedin.com/in/revanth-mudavath

## Personalization Tips (for agent)
- Research the company first: what do they build, what did they ship recently?
- Match bullets to the role: backend role -> API/DB bullets; full-stack -> SpeedScore bullets
- The hook in line 1 is most important — make it specific, not generic
- Never say "I saw your job posting" — reference the role naturally
- Count words in the body carefully: 120-180 is a hard rule
- Avoid em dashes (—), use commas or periods instead
- Avoid double hyphens (--), use a comma or new sentence instead
