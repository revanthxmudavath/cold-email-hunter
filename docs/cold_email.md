# Cold Email Writing Guide

## Rules
- Subject: 7 words or fewer, role-specific (e.g. "Backend Engineer role at Stripe")
- Body: 130–150 words total — count carefully, no exceptions
- Tone: direct, professional, fully human — never mention AI or automation
- Every email MUST include the exact job URL from the job posting
- NO em dashes (—) anywhere in the email; use a comma, period, or rewrite instead
- NO special characters that look odd in plain text email clients
- ALWAYS start with "Hi [First Name]," on its own line; never skip the salutation

## Template

Subject: [Role] at [Company]

Hi [First Name],

I'm Revanth, an MS CS student at Oregon State graduating June 2026. I've been following [Company] for a while and loved reading about [specific product / solution / practice they build or shipped recently]. Given my background in [area relevant to JD/company], I've always felt [Company] would be a great fit and I love how closely it aligns with what I've been building.

I recently came across your opening for [Role] in [Location] and wanted to reach out personally.

- [Achievement 1 — matched to THIS specific JD]
- [Achievement 2 — matched to THIS specific JD]
- [Achievement 3 — matched to THIS specific JD, optional]

Role I'm interested in: [Role]
[Exact job URL]

I'm attaching my resume, but I truly believe my experience maps closely to what this role needs. I'd love to speak further if you think I could be a good fit.

Best,
Revanth Mudavath
linkedin: linkedin.com/in/revanth-mudavath

---

## Personalization Tips (for agent)
- Intro is fixed — do not change the first sentence
- Company hook (sentence 2) is most important — make it specific, not generic
- Research the company first: what do they build, what did they ship recently?
- Match bullets to the role: backend role -> API/DB/latency bullets; full-stack -> coverage/testing bullets
- Pick only 2–3 bullets — choose the ones most relevant to the JD, not all 4
- The "area relevant to JD/company" in paragraph 2 should match the role (e.g. "backend systems", "full-stack development")
- Never say "I saw your job posting" — use "I recently came across your opening"
- Count words in the body carefully: 130–150 is a hard rule
- Avoid em dashes (—), use commas or periods instead
- Avoid double hyphens (--), use a comma or new sentence instead
- Resume is always attached — do not change the closing line
- Paragraph 1 = intro sentence + company hook sentence + background/fit sentence — all one paragraph, no blank line between them
