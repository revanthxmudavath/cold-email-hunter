📩 How I Landed a Job by Sending One Cold Email (And You Can Too)
I landed my new job by sending a cold email to the head of recruiting — and this exact structure has helped me get countless interviews. If you follow it, I can almost guarantee it will work.

1️⃣ Subject Line – Your Make-or-Break Moment
Why it matters:
The subject line is what decides if your email gets opened or ignored.
Think of it like the first impression — short, clear, and professional.
Options that work:
Straightforward: Interested in [Role] at [Company]
Creative but professional: Do this to 10x your Sales!
Pro tip:
Avoid vague subject lines like “Hello” or “Job Inquiry” — they get lost in a recruiter’s inbox.
Keep it ≤7 words if possible — clear and scannable.







2️⃣ The Body – Keep It Brief & On-Point
Divide the email into three core parts:

Part A: Warm Introduction
One line about who you are. Example:
“Hi [Name], I’m [Your Name], a Product Strategist with experience in [Skill/Industry].”
Then, show you’re a fit for the company:
Mention a product you’ve used, a project you admire, or something the company recently shipped.
This shows you did your research and care about the company specifically.
Pro tip:
Small personal touches stand out. Don’t just say “I like your company” — highlight a concrete detail.

Part B: Relevant Work Experience (3–4 Bullet Points)
Highlight measurable achievements and skills related to the role.
Example:
Launched X feature, resulting in Y% increase in engagement
Improved process Z, saving N hours per week
Keep bullets concise and results-oriented. Recruiters scan quickly.
Why this works:
Recruiters can see your fit in seconds.
Shows you’re already contributing at the level they need.

Part C: Roles You’re Interested In
List the roles you’re targeting with job links attached.
Example:
Product Strategist – [Job Link]
GTM / Solutions Engineer – [Job Link]
Pro tip:
Only include roles you genuinely fit — quality over quantity.
3️⃣ Close With a Clear Call to Action
End the email with a polite and actionable CTA.
Example: “Would love to chat about this role — happy to share my resume.”
Attach your resume.
Keep it simple:
Brief, personalized, and shows initiative.

💡 Extra Tips for Success
Send multiple emails: The job market is competitive — volume matters.
Track responses: Keep a spreadsheet of emails sent, follow-ups, and replies.
Follow up politely: 3–5 days later is perfect if you don’t hear back.
Tailor every email: Never send a generic template — personalization is key.
